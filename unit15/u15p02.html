Step 1: Make it easier

The first step in the process is to make your schematic easier to read. Though this is possible to do with a document file, I find it much easier to do the entire schematic analysis project with a printed copy of the schematic. The larger the better.

In figure 2-1 you see an example of this first step. I have made ground and the positive voltage easier to identify by color coding them. I have extended the color coding up until the start of a component. This makes it easier to see, even with a quick glance, where +V and GND are on the schematic. This circuit does not have any -V but if your circuit does, that should be highlighted as well. In addition, any voltages that are sent around the circuit are worth highlighting if you can identify them at this point. If you identify them later in the process it would be worth adding color coding for them. for instance the control voltage for a compressor.
