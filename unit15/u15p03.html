Step 2: What is this stuff?

The second step may seem too simple to worry about, but it is worth doing at this point and should not take very much time. You want to go through every component in the schematic and identify it, listing the basic properties of that component.

The table shows this component listing for the preamp portion of the 1176 limiter shown in figure 3-1. Your version of this table will serve as a reference for you as you work on your schematic. it is something you should have printed out next to y our printed schematic.

Component Component IDs Description Rules
Resistors
 R6, R7 T8, R9, R10, R11, R12, R13, R14, R15, R17, R18, R19, R20, R21

drops voltage with current flow
  • $ {V}={I}{R} $
  • series resistors add $ R_{total} = R_1 + R_2 + R_3 + ..... $
  • Parallel resistors add $ \frac {1}{R_{total}} = \frac {1}{R_1} + \frac {1}{R_2} + \frac {1}{R_3} ..... $
Potentiometer R16

resistor with adjustable wiper

Wiper moves toward CCW when turned counterclockwise and toward CW when turned clockwise

Think of as a voltage divider.
Capacitors

 C1, C2, C3, C4, C5, C6, C7, C8, C9, C27

drops different voltage for different frequencies

 Reactance $x_{c} = \frac {1}{2\pi FC} $
NPN Transistor
 Q2, Q3, Q4 small current into base controls large current from collector to emitter
  • 5% current at base, 95% at emitter, 100% at collector
  • Emitter must be most negative,
  • The closer the base voltage is to the collector the larger the current, the closer to the emitter the smaller the current
JFET
 Q1 voltage at gate controls current from drain to source, drain and source are reversible Voltage at gate (more negative than Source) reduces current flow between source and drain
