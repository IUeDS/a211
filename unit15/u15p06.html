Step 5: What happens?

The last step can be very different depending on the type of circuit.

In our example portion of the 1176, the signal preamplifier, the signal is just amplified. That means there is not much to do for this last step of "analyze with input signal". For the compressor circuit as a whole, however, this is a lot to do.

Here are some thoughts on how you might approach this for different types of circuits (just a start).

Device type Questions to ask/
Experiments		 notes
Compressor (or other dynamics)
  • What happens with loud signal?
  • What happens with soft signal?
 The basic concept of a compressor is that there is a path for audio and a separate path that controls the gain reduction. This gain reduction control path typically has audio flowing to it and DC flowing out of it.
Equalizer
  • What happens with high frequencies?
  • What happens with midrange frequencies?
  • What happens (can you guess this question?) with low frequencies?
 The paths for different frequency ranges may be similar. You may find that once you understand the high frequency path, for instance, that the low frequency path is easily understood.
Microphone How do things change when polarity is changed? Most microphone circuits will have the components that create the voltage across the diaphragm and the components for the audio path together. they will not be visually separated on the schematic.
Discrete preamplifier or power amplifier
  • What happens to DC?
  • What happens to AC?
  • When are the paths of AC and DC different?
