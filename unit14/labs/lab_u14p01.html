Question #3: How many dB of gain is that?

Correct, you get a gold star.
A review of the dB formula might be necessary.
Decibel (dB): A logarithmic way to define values. This is used a great deal with audio as it emulates the way that human beings hear. For this course we will just use it for defining the slope of filters. The math is actually not too complex. To find a dB value you need to compare two things. Often that is a set reference value (1V for dBV for instance). For slope on filters you would compare the voltage at a frequency with the voltage at another frequency. The formula when you are starting with voltage is $dB = 20log(\frac {V1}{V2})$

Break it

14-5. You are now going to remove R2 from the circuit entirely. This will give you an R2 value of ∞ or infinity. This will make the gain of your circuit infinity (or some real world number that is close to that). Does the world end?
No, though gain is infinite the output of your OpAmp is still constrained (just like very other circuit you have built) by the voltage supplied to it. The maximum output will be less than 30Vpk-pk.

14-6. So what happens when you have infinite gain but a limited maximum output?
You get distortion, lots of distortion. Try it now. Try it with the sine wave and oscilloscope (you will need to use the "x10" probe setting in order to measure that much voltage).
